Both Java and C++ have separate use cases. I did C++ because I had to study C in university as well, therefore, I focussed on C++ instead. It is faster in some cases when undertaking competitive programming. Java on the other hand can also be used for Android App Development in addition to problem solving. In our university, there are literally societies for everything! Be it technical, or non-technical, there is not a single society on campus where you won't have fun. When you are joining a society, make sure you are going to "stay" rather than just being there for the sake of it. As for the question of what kind of societies should you join, I suggest joining at-least one technical society and then choosing other societies if you want. Do attend the orientations of the societies to know which society will be the best for you.
